What is a ratio?
  • Examples
  • Questions
    • Keypoint
    A ratio states the comparison between two or more parts within a whole. The parts may be different or equal in size. The notation for a ratio uses a colon to separate the numeric parts, eg 5 : 2 Ratios are usually written using integers, but not always. Ratios may be simplified by dividing the parts by their highest common factor (HCF). The ratio 10 : 4 simplifies to 5 : 2, for example. To simplify ratios, knowledge of how to find the highest common factor (HCF) is useful.
